Map Information

This map displays at-risk-of-poverty rates across European countries.

A lighter shade of blue represents a lower at-risk-of-poverty rate, and a darker shade of blue indicates a higher at-risk-of-poverty rate. The grey color represents the countries with no accessible data.

Use the dropdown menu to select a year and watch the map change.

What is at-risk-of-poverty rate? The share of persons with an equivalised disposable income below the risk-of-poverty threshold, which is set at 60% of the national median equivalised disposable income (after social transfers).

Line Chart Information

This line chart displays the poverty risk rate for men and women.

You can select a country and compare it to the European Union average.

The chart covers the period from 2010 to 2023.

The blue line represents the male poverty rate for the EU total, while the red line shows the female poverty rate for the EU total.

The green and orange lines display the corresponding poverty rates for women and men in the chosen country.

Bar Chart Information

This bar chart displays the daily median income across various countries, measured in international dollars.

The X-axis displays the countries in ascending order of daily income.

The Y-axis represents median income pr. day.
